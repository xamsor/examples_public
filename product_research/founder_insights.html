Core Finding

FatGrid has a retention problem, not a conversion problem. With 17% monthly churn, the business is on a treadmill—new subscribers replace churned ones, resulting in zero net growth since November 2025.

The Product

FatGrid is a B2B SaaS tool for link-building professionals. It aggregates pricing data from ~20 major link-building marketplaces, enabling users to find the best prices for guest posts and link insertions.

Think of it as "Kayak for backlink purchases"—price comparison across a fragmented marketplace ecosystem.

Two Different Products

Analysis revealed that Business and Standard customers have fundamentally different use cases:

Business Tier ($159/mo)

Who: Marketplaces monitoring competitors, large agencies integrating via API

Use case: Competitive intelligence, contract enforcement, data integration

Churn sensitivity to data accuracy: Low

Retention: 2x longer than Standard

Standard Tier ($29/mo)

Who: SEO professionals, freelancers, small agencies

Use case: Find and buy cheaper links via price comparison

Churn sensitivity to data accuracy: High

Problem: One stale price = lost trust

Key Insight

Business customers don't care as much about price accuracy—they need market breadth for intelligence. Standard customers depend on accuracy to make purchasing decisions. Same product, different jobs-to-be-done, different churn profiles.

Why Standard Users Churn ASSUMPTION - NEEDS VALIDATION

The hypothesized churn driver is data accuracy. When aggregating lowest prices across marketplaces, FatGrid also aggregates errors:

  • Publishers raise prices, some marketplaces update slowly
  • The "lowest price" is often outdated
  • Users hit errors more often than working with a single marketplace directly
  • Additional friction: must register on each marketplace to place orders
"The main reason for churn is data accuracy. When we aggregate the lowest prices, we also aggregate errors."
— Founder
Important

This hypothesis has not been validated with churned user interviews. The founder should talk to 5 recently churned users to confirm or discover the real reasons.

Strategic Recommendations

1
Validate the Churn Hypothesis

Talk to 5 recently churned users. Offer $25 gift card for 15 minutes. Ask: "What was the moment you decided to cancel?" This will either confirm data accuracy is the issue or reveal something unexpected.

2
Interview Long-Tenure Customers

Your 38 Standard customers averaging 117 days are the "survivors." Ask them: "Why are you still here when others left?" Their answers may reveal the real value proposition.

3
Consider Focusing on Business Tier

Business customers retain 2x longer, pay 5x more, and have a use case less sensitive to data accuracy. 10 more Business customers = +$1,590/mo = 67% MRR growth. These come from LinkedIn—can you do more of what's already working?

4
Pause the Starter Plan

Don't add complexity while the foundation leaks. Fix retention first, then revisit cheaper tiers.

5
Delay Competitor Acquisition

At current churn rates, you'd just process their users through a leaky funnel. Fix retention first—then the $50k acquisition becomes much more valuable.
